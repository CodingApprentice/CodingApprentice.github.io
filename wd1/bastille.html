Bastille

In Paris there was a huge fortress and prison called the Bastille where people were locked up for years without any form of trial. To the people of Paris it symbolised tyranny. It also contains a large supply of gunpowder, and fearing an attack from the army, the people of Paris needed Arms.

On July 14th 1789 a mob of workers, crafts people and small business owners from the district attacked the Bastille. These people were known as without breeches because they were poor and wore trousers instead of the expensive breeches then in fashion.

After a confused attack in which almost 100 of them were killed, negotiations with the Bastille defenders took place. Meanwhile, the fall of the Bastille had a huge impact. Most of Paris rose now in rebellion against the king. The leader of the National assembly was elected mayor of Paris. On July 17th King Louis XVI was compelled to come to Paris and put on the type of hat worn by those who had stormed the Bastille. On the Hat there was a rosette of red, white and blue Ribbons. The assembly had won a great victory. But the French Revolution was not over. It has only just begun.
